    4. Insights & Recommendations

    Insights:


  • High volume of pending inspections localized in Pali, Hanumangadh, Jaisalmer, and Jaipur.

  • Longest delays (>200 days) mostly linked to specific dealers (Ramprasad Prajapat, Rai Singh).

  • All inspections remain unperformed despite many PV Form submissions - indicating bottleneck in inspection scheduling/execution.

  • PV Form "Not Given" cases form a significant portion, showing incomplete data/input from dealers or customers.

  • Inconsistent data entries (district and dealer names) may hinder proper filtering and timely follow-up.

  • Dealer communication info incomplete for Hanumangadh, Dausa, some others - impacting coordination.

    • Some reasons for delay noted in remarks (missing documents, weather), but mostly not recorded limiting root cause analysis.


    Recommendations:


  • Data Cleanup: Standardize district and dealer names and ensure clean, consistent entries.

  • Improve Data Completeness: Mandate key fields like dealer email, inspection dates once done, and explicit remarks on pending cases.

  • Prioritization: Flag highest delay cases (>150 days) for urgent inspection scheduling.

  • Follow-up on PV Form Status: Ensure stakeholders complete PV form submissions to avoid delays.

  • Focused inspection drives in top delay-heavy districts (Pali, Hanumangadh, Jaipur) and dealers with largest pending volumes.

  • Audit remarks: Encourage dealers to note detailed reasons for delays to facilitate future analysis.

  • Exclude metadata entries from operational datasets to avoid skewing reporting.

  • Automation or alerts: Build alerts based on delay_days to prompt action when delays cross thresholds (e.g., 90 days).
